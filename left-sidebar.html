Latest News

Recent milestones, pilots, and upcoming developments from Myconoko.

MVP Successfully Developed

Myconoko has completed its MVP, automating core controls and successfully growing 10 types of mushrooms — an important step toward a scalable urban farming system.

Farmacy Pilot Farm Sponsored

Farmacy has sponsored a pilot mobile mushroom farm at their farm site, helping Myconoko test the system in a real-world environment.

Supplying SEED Restaurant

Myconoko is producing mushrooms to order for SEED restaurant, showing early commercial traction for its locally grown gourmet mushrooms.

Community & Education Projects

A community farm for SEN students is planned for mid-2026, and a mushroom terrarium project for an art group is scheduled for March to May 2026. The team has also received strong interest from schools looking to set up education and experiential farms.

What’s Coming Next

Over the next 1–2 years, Myconoko plans to build MMF 2.0, develop its patented spore filtration unit, pilot more farms in schools and urban locations, expand substrate production, and run more public education campaigns and workshops.

Pilot Partnerships

From Farmacy to restaurant collaborations, Myconoko is testing its system through real-world pilots and early customer partnerships.
